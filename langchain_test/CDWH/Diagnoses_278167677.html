DataWarehouse : Diagnoses

Created by Fabian Franzeck on Okt. 03, 2023

This site gives information regarding principal aspects of analysis of Diagnosis data

Existing entities

  1. V_IL_FCT_DIAGNOSIS_ISMED

  2. V_IL_FCT_DIAGNOSIS

There are two ways how diagnoses are recorded in daily practice:

  1. During the treatment episode (outpatient and inpatient), physicians document diagnoses in freetext in ISMED/Meona. Those entries are found in V_IL_FCT_DIAGNOSIS_ISMED and can details regarding analysis are described here: ISMed Diagnosis Model . In Short, this is the most meaningful clinical data, but it is freetext and needs a lot of effort to analyse properly, which often ist not fully automatable. There are a lot of specific case to consider, as not everything represents a final diagnosis: There are also things like ‘Verdacht auf’, ‘Ausschluss von’, etc. which all needs to be considered. The Timepoint when a diagnosis was made is also only contained in the freetext, not in structured date fields (there are some in the entity, but don’t use them, as they are purely technical and misleading!)

  2. After an inpatient case (cas_type = 'stationär' in DIM_CASE), diagnoses relevant for billing (but not all diangoses listed in ISMED!) are coded by the billing/controlling team. They might also code Diagnoses, that are not explicitely mentioned by clinicians in ISMED: it’s all about billing optimisation! I.e. those diagnoses are not necessarily so meaningful or complete from a clinical or research perspective, but they are well suited for analytical procedures as they done using ICD-10 codes.There are also multiple Timestamps in the entity, however they don’t typically reflect the true date when a diagnosis was made! DIG_DATE_TS is in 95% of diagnosis simply set to the first calender date of the case, DIG_CREATE_DATE_TS is the timestamp, when the medical coder coded this diagnosis which is typically after the patient left the hospital
