Project description

Swisscare is the registry of the Schweizer Gesellschaft für Kardiologie collecting data on coronary artery catheterisations.

The datamanagement is outsourced to SwissRDL: Projectwebsite

Description from the website: SwissCaRe wurde 2022 durch die Schweizerische Gesellschaft für Kardiologie (SGK) und deren Arbeitsgruppe für interventionelle Kardiologie ins Leben gerufen, um den steigenden Anforderungen an medizinische Qualitätssicherung und -verbesserung von Seiten der Ärzteschaft, Behörden und Gesellschaft gerecht zu werden. Bei der SGK handelt es sich um den Berufsverband der Schweizer Kardiologen und einen wissenschaftlichen Verein gemäss Art. 60 ff. SwissCaRe wird von SwissRDL im Auftrag der SGK geführt. Bei SwissRDL handelt es sich um einen Dienstleister des Instituts für Sozial- und Präventivmedizin der Universität Bern, welches grosse Erfahrung im Führen medizinischer Register besitzt.

    An angiography (= LIKA) without PCI (= PTCA) on the same calender or within 4h after angiography (sometimes the angiography is before midnight and the PCI after midnight) is classified as '1'

    Info Michael

    Derivation Logic for IV_TYPE

    At USB, Lika and ptca are documentd as two distinct examinations with no clear link between them, even when they are done together in one session. Therefore we need to derive which exams belong together.

    Lika is a diagnostic exam, ptca a therapeutic intervention after lika and they are often done together in one session in the intervention room. When done together, logically first is lika then ptca.  For Swisscare, exams need to be classified if 1) it is just a lika 2) lika + ptca 3) just a ptca (Parameter IV_TYPE).

    Typically, there is one lika and one ptca per calenderday. Theoretically the lika can also be before midnight and the ptca after midnight (emergencies at night) and those should be associated together still. Therefore, we cant just put them together using calender dates but also need to use a reasonable timeinterval to link them over 2 adjacent calenderdates (4hours)

     

    We have to artificially classify the relationships between lika and ptca. There can be multiple likas and multiple ptcas per day with numbers not necessarily matching. However, one lika can should only be matched once to ptca and vice versa. Also the order is not necessarily documented in a correct manner (eg ptca can have an earlier timestamp in the data than lika, even though the real order was the other way around). This creates problems in the classification we need to do, particularly when there are multiples per day: The logical order is lost and automatic interpretation becomes very complex and we will not do that.

     

    Thus I defined the logic for classification of IV_TYPE:

    1. if there is exactly one lika and exactly one ptca on one calender day, we associate them together, no matter their order!!: IV_TYPE =2

    2. If there is lika only per calender date and no ptca within 4h of lika: we classify as IV_TYPE =1

    3. If there is only a PTCA on a day and no Lika within 4h preceding the ptca, then we classify as IV_TYPE=3

    4. When there are multiple Likas per day, they associate to the closest following ptca on the same day or within 4h of lika. if there is two likas, eg. one at 1pm and 5pm and a ptca at 6pm, the lika at 1pm is classified as iv_type=1 and  the lika  at 5pm is associated with the ptca at 6pm to be classified as iv_type=2

    5. if there are multiple ptcas and less likas, eg a lika at 4pm, and ptca at 2pm and 6pm: the ptca at 2 pm is classified as iv_type =3 and the lika at 4 pm is associated with the ptca at 6pm to be classified as IV_TYPE=2

     

    Obviously, order plays a matter in this logic (if there is not just one lika and one ptca per day) and the cardiologists know this needs to documented right in order for this to work properly. However, even if the order is not documented correctly (which is the case sometimes), this is only a minor issue, as it is a rare case anyway

     

     

     

    Pseudonymisation in declined consents

    In the meeting with Luisa Schäfer, M. Zellweger and F. Franzeck from July 20th 2023, it was decided by M. Zellweger:

    There are 3 possible consent status (specific consent for this registry):

    1. Nein

    2. Ja

    3. Notfall

    In case of Nein: Names are changed to “consent_declined”, Date of birth is changed to the January 1st of the same calender year as the patients birthdate, PAT_BK is changed to a peppered HASH of the original PAT_BK (i.e. adding a fixed string to the pat_bk and the then run the hash function) - the hashed pat_bk needs to be stable over time so the information how many exams a patient had is still retained.

    In case of Ja/Notfall: All information are sent (specifically the “Notfall” ones do need accept consent, but it was decided to treat them as “Ja”
